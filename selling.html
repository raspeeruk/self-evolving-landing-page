For Manufacturing Clients

How our solutions deliver measurable value to your operations

Bridging the Gap Between Operations and Management

Manufacturing plants generate enormous amounts of valuable data, but this data often remains trapped in silos or goes unused. Our solutions bridge the gap between your plant floor operations and management decision-making.

Quantify the Financial Impact of Operational Metrics

Transform technical metrics into financial insights that speak the language of management:

  • Convert downtime hours into direct financial loss
  • Translate energy consumption into cost per unit produced
  • Quantify the impact of quality issues on the bottom line
  • Calculate the ROI of process improvements

Example:

A galvanization plant was experiencing 50 hours of downtime annually on a critical line. By quantifying this as €50,000 in lost production and implementing targeted improvements, downtime was reduced by 60%, resulting in €30,000 annual savings.

From Data to Actionable Insights

Move beyond raw data to actionable intelligence that drives decision-making:

  • Identify patterns and trends that aren't visible in daily operations
  • Receive proactive alerts before issues become critical
  • Compare performance across different production lines or facilities
  • Correlate multiple factors to identify root causes of issues

Example:

A manufacturing client was struggling with inconsistent product quality. Our dashboard revealed a correlation between quality issues and specific temperature patterns that weren't obvious in the raw data. This insight led to process adjustments that improved quality by 23%.

Eliminate Manual Reporting and Analysis

Free up valuable time for your technical and management teams:

  • Automate data collection and report generation
  • Eliminate error-prone manual data entry
  • Ensure consistent methodology in calculations and analysis
  • Deliver insights to the right people at the right time

Example:

An analyst at a client company was spending 15 hours per week collecting data from various systems, manipulating it in Excel, and creating reports. By automating this process, we freed up nearly 40% of their time for higher-value analysis and improvement initiatives.

How to Present These Solutions to Your Clients

As an industrial automation expert, you're already trusted by your clients for your technical expertise. Here's how to effectively introduce these business intelligence solutions:

1. Start with Observation

During your regular work with clients, look for these common indicators that they could benefit from our solutions:

  • Manual Excel-based reporting processes
  • Lack of visibility into plant performance metrics
  • Disconnect between plant floor data and management reporting
  • Recurring issues that could be prevented with better monitoring
  • Excessive alarm notifications or missed critical alarms

2. Ask Targeted Questions

These questions can help uncover specific pain points and opportunities:

  • "How do you currently track and analyze your plant's performance data?"
  • "How much time does your team spend collecting data and creating reports?"
  • "Do you have visibility into the financial impact of downtime or quality issues?"
  • "How quickly can you identify and respond to emerging issues in your operations?"
  • "What operational metrics would you like better visibility into?"

3. Present a Specific Example

Rather than discussing abstract capabilities, share a concrete example relevant to their industry:

  • Show a sample dashboard with metrics specific to their operations
  • Describe a similar client's success story (anonymized as needed)
  • Explain how a specific pain point they've mentioned could be addressed
  • Offer to create a simple proof of concept using their actual data

4. Focus on Business Outcomes

Emphasize the business value rather than technical features:

  • Quantify potential cost savings or efficiency improvements
  • Highlight time saved by automating manual processes
  • Discuss improved decision-making through better visibility
  • Explain how the solution complements your existing automation work

5. Propose a Low-Risk Starting Point

Make it easy for clients to say yes by starting small:

  • Suggest a focused proof of concept addressing one specific pain point
  • Offer a workshop to identify high-value opportunities
  • Propose integrating with just one data source initially
  • Emphasize the iterative approach that delivers value at each stage
